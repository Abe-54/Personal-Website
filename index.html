Storytelling in Gaming Podcast


Artist Statement: The main goal of creating this podcast is to explore what storytelling is when it comes to video games and to learn a little more about Raymond Arriagada. As he is a senior in game design, I wanted to get his perspective on game development and how he approached it. This podcast is designed to bring to the surface the details of some modern games. I used coin effects to divide the podcast into different sections and to give it a more video game type feel to it. In addition to sound effects, I incorporated narration to parts of the interview in order to let the listener understand what we were talking about.

Puerto Rico Ruins in 60


Artist Statement: In this short video, I chose to explore some ruins I visited during a recent trip to Puerto Rico. As a game developer, I enjoy taking inspiration from real-world locations to add to my games. The goals were to inspire appreciation of these types of locations and see what has become of them over the years. In the video, I decided to use up beat tropical music to add a sense of awe to the clip and allow the viewer to feel what I felt when I first explored these ruins. Furthermore, I decided to open up with a shot of the beach on a clear day in order to set the scene of being in a tropical island. Overall, this was an amazing experience and it will inspire all my future projects.

Client Project - Literary Magazine


Artist Statement:The main goal for the client project was to improve upon last year’s website’s design and showcase the student body’s work in one easy to access central location.

As I was working on this project, I decided to use the previous website as inspiration to design this year's website. I discussed with my team and the client to ensure the project was what they desired. After looking at last year’s website, I made sure to take the parts that I thought worked and removed what I thought did not. All of this led to the final design of the website.

My process to capture content for the website was to first decide on the layout of each page on the website. Once I had a rough layout of the page, I’d go in and add some color and ensure it had the theme that the client requested. Then I’d ensure that the student content was going to be easily accessible and displayed. Finally, I’d use the feedback I got from the usability tests and implement the suggestions I received.
